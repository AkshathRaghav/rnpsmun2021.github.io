UNSC

“Peace is not absence of conflict, it is the ability to handle conflict by peaceful means."

Ronald Reagan


United Nation Security Council’s biggest challenge has been dealing with conflicts – between or within states – without offending the national sovereignty of its member states. A daunting task that lies ahead of you - to intervene or not to intervene in a nation’s internal affairs. Countries like Bolivia, Yemen, Syria and other states with exigent humanitarian crises that arise out of internal conflicts require the intervention of the UNSC and its members. The Israel-Palestine ongoing conflict resulted in Palestine ultimately sending a plea to the UN for its intervention. The UN allows the Council to "determine the existence of any threat to the peace, breach of the peace, or act of aggression" and to take military and nonmilitary action to "restore international peace and security". (According to the Chapter VII of UN Charters). Sovereignty is a power and a responsibility; member States of the UN have exploited the Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) and their sovereignty and regional arrangement power. The aim of this committee is to highlight the responsibility of sovereignty, the obligation of developed countries and to discuss reforms in the UNSC.
